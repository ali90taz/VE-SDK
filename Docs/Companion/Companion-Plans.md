<h2 align="center">
Plans for the VitaEngine Companion
</br>
<br>
</h2>

<h4 align="left">
The <b>VitaEngine Companion</b> is intended to be more than a simple receiver 
application.
<br>
<br>
Its long-term role is to act as the <b>device-side companion application</b> of 
the VitaEngine ecosystem, helping bridge the gap between desktop development and 
execution on real <b>PlayStation Vita</b> hardware.
<br>
<br>
This document exists as a <b>long-term implementation reference</b> and outlines 
the intended direction of the Companion as the platform evolves.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Important note</h3>

<h4 align="left">
The features described here represent <b>long-term plans and design goals</b>.
<br>
<br>
Their presence in this document <b>does not imply immediate availability</b>, 
fixed milestones, guaranteed implementation, or a strict roadmap.
<br>
<br>
This file is meant to document the <b>intended vision</b> of the Companion and 
help preserve architectural consistency over time.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Core vision</h3>

<h4 align="left">
The VitaEngine Companion is planned as a <b>PS Vita application</b> focused on 
the development workflow of projects created with <b>VitaEngine</b>.
<br>
<br>
Its purpose is not to replace community tools, but to provide a more 
<b>integrated</b>, <b>practical</b> and <b>development-oriented</b> workflow 
between the desktop IDE and real hardware.
<br>
<br>
In long-term terms, the Companion is expected to become a:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li><b>Preview target</b> for projects under development</li>
  <li><b>Deployment receiver</b> for development and packaged builds</li>
  <li><b>Lightweight lifecycle manager</b> for VitaEngine-generated applications</li>
  <li><b>Support utility</b> for iteration and future save handling workflows</li>
</ul>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Design philosophy</h3>

<h4 align="left">
The Companion is intended to follow a <b>focused scope</b>.
<br>
<br>
It should:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li><b>Complement</b>, not replace, tools such as <b>VitaShell</b></li>
  <li>Prioritize <b>developer convenience</b> for VitaEngine projects</li>
  <li>Avoid becoming a <b>generic package manager</b> or broad system utility</li>
  <li>Remain tightly aligned with the <b>VitaEngine ecosystem</b></li>
</ul>
<br>
Because of that, Companion-managed operations are expected to be limited to 
<b>applications generated by VitaEngine</b> whenever possible.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Long-term responsibilities</h3>

<h4 align="left">
Over time, the VitaEngine Companion may evolve to support responsibilities such 
as:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>Receiving <b>development bundles</b> sent from the VitaEngine IDE</li>
  <li>Running <b>preview</b> or <b>development builds</b> directly on hardware</li>
  <li>Receiving packaged <b>.VPK</b> files generated by VitaEngine</li>
  <li>Validating whether a package belongs to the <b>VitaEngine ecosystem</b></li>
  <li>Installing supported <b>VitaEngine-generated .VPK</b> packages</li>
  <li>Launching installed <b>VitaEngine applications</b></li>
  <li>Re-launching recently deployed builds for faster iteration</li>
</ul>
<br>
These responsibilities form the basis of the Companion’s intended role as a 
practical extension of the desktop workflow on real hardware.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Planned workflow integration</h3>

<h4 align="left">
One of the main long-term goals is to allow the <b>VitaEngine IDE</b> and the 
<b>VitaEngine Companion</b> to work together as part of a more integrated 
deployment pipeline.
<br>
<br>
Potential supported flows may include:
<br>
<br>
</h4>

<h4>Development preview flow</h4>

<h4 align="left">
<ul>
  <li>Build development bundle on desktop</li>
  <li>Send bundle to Companion</li>
  <li>Run directly inside the Companion environment</li>
  <li>Potentially support faster iteration-oriented reload flows in future stages</li>
</ul>
<br>
</h4>

<h4>Packaged deployment flow</h4>

<h4 align="left">
<ul>
  <li>Build project</li>
  <li>Package <b>.VPK</b></li>
  <li>Send <b>.VPK</b> to Companion</li>
  <li>Validate package origin and compatibility</li>
  <li>Install on device</li>
  <li>Optionally launch immediately after installation</li>
</ul>
<br>
This approach is intended to reduce friction during testing while still 
preserving compatibility with traditional manual workflows when desired.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Companion-managed package installation</h3>

<h4 align="left">
A key long-term convenience goal is to allow the Companion to support 
installation of <b>.VPK</b> packages generated by VitaEngine.
<br>
<br>
This is not intended to replace manual installation methods, but rather to 
provide an <b>optional integrated workflow</b> for developers who want a smoother 
deploy experience directly from the IDE.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

<h4>Scope limitation</h4>

<h4 align="left">
The Companion is <b>not intended</b> to become a universal installer for 
arbitrary packages.
<br>
<br>
Instead, installation support is expected to remain focused on:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li><b>.VPK</b> files generated by VitaEngine</li>
  <li>Packages that can be identified as part of the <b>VitaEngine ecosystem</b></li>
  <li>Development and testing workflows related to VitaEngine projects</li>
</ul>
<br>
This limitation helps preserve the intended scope and keeps the Companion aligned 
with its role as an ecosystem-specific tool.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Future application lifecycle management</h3>

<h4 align="left">
Beyond installation and execution, the Companion may eventually support broader 
lifecycle management for <b>VitaEngine-generated applications</b>.
<br>
<br>
Possible future features may include:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>Listing installed VitaEngine applications</li>
  <li>Uninstalling VitaEngine applications</li>
  <li>Detecting previously deployed builds</li>
  <li>Showing basic metadata such as <b>title</b>, <b>version</b> or <b>build type</b></li>
  <li>Managing fast re-deployment during iteration</li>
</ul>
<br>
This would allow the Companion to serve as a lightweight manager for 
VitaEngine applications on real hardware.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Future save data management</h3>

<h4 align="left">
Another long-term possibility is support for <b>save data handling</b> for 
VitaEngine-generated applications.
<br>
<br>
Potential future capabilities may include:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>Exporting save data</li>
  <li>Importing or restoring save data</li>
  <li>Preserving saves across test builds</li>
  <li>Resetting save data during development</li>
  <li>Supporting safer iteration on projects that depend on persistent state</li>
</ul>
<br>
This may be especially useful for developers testing:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>Gameplay progression</li>
  <li>Settings systems</li>
  <li>Persistent state logic</li>
  <li>Compatibility across multiple builds</li>
</ul>
<br>
As with other Companion features, save management is intended only for 
<b>applications created within the VitaEngine ecosystem</b>.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Ecosystem identity and validation</h3>

<h4 align="left">
To support safe and predictable Companion-managed workflows, VitaEngine-generated 
applications may eventually include identifiable metadata that allows the 
Companion to recognize them as valid ecosystem packages.
<br>
<br>
This may be implemented through:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>Embedded package metadata</li>
  <li>A dedicated <b>VitaEngine manifest</b></li>
  <li>Build identifiers</li>
  <li>Version and compatibility information</li>
</ul>
<br>
Such metadata would help the Companion determine whether a package is eligible 
for:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>Installation</li>
  <li>Lifecycle management</li>
  <li>Save management</li>
  <li>Future ecosystem-specific features</li>
</ul>
<br>
This is expected to be an important part of keeping the Companion focused, 
predictable and aligned with the platform.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>What the Companion is <i>not</i> intended to be</h3>

<h4 align="left">
To keep its purpose clear, the Companion is currently <b>not intended</b> to 
become:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>A replacement for <b>VitaShell</b></li>
  <li>A generic <b>.VPK</b> installer for all homebrew</li>
  <li>A full system management tool</li>
  <li>A broad file browser</li>
  <li>A general-purpose package manager outside the VitaEngine ecosystem</li>
</ul>
<br>
Its role is intentionally narrower:
<br>
<br>
<i>A focused companion application designed to improve the development, 
deployment and testing workflow for VitaEngine projects on real hardware.</i>
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Implementation philosophy</h3>

<h4 align="left">
The VitaEngine Companion is planned as a <b>long-term component</b> and should 
be developed incrementally.
<br>
<br>
Features may be introduced in stages, prioritizing <b>practicality</b> and 
<b>reliability</b> over feature count.
<br>
<br>
A reasonable evolution path may look like:
<br>
<br>
</h4>

<h4>Early stage</h4>

<h4 align="left">
<ul>
  <li>Receive development bundles</li>
  <li>Run preview builds</li>
  <li>Receive packaged builds</li>
  <li>Launch test sessions</li>
</ul>
<br>
</h4>

<h4>Mid stage</h4>

<h4 align="left">
<ul>
  <li>Install VitaEngine-generated <b>.VPK</b> packages</li>
  <li>Re-launch builds more conveniently</li>
  <li>Improve communication with the desktop IDE</li>
</ul>
<br>
</h4>

<h4>Later stage</h4>

<h4 align="left">
<ul>
  <li>Manage installed VitaEngine applications</li>
  <li>Uninstall supported applications</li>
  <li>Support save data export/import workflows</li>
  <li>Expand validation and metadata-driven behavior</li>
</ul>
<br>
This staged approach helps keep the project realistic while preserving the 
long-term vision.
<br>
<br>
</h4>

---

<h3>Final note</h3>

<h4 align="left">
The VitaEngine Companion is envisioned as an important part of the broader 
<b>VitaEngine experience</b>.
<br>
<br>
Its long-term purpose is not simply to <i>receive files</i>, but to become a 
meaningful extension of the desktop environment on real <b>PlayStation Vita</b> 
hardware:
<br>
<br>
<ul>
  <li>A <b>preview target</b></li>
  <li>A <b>deployment receiver</b></li>
  <li>An installer for <b>VitaEngine-generated packages</b></li>
  <li>A lightweight <b>lifecycle manager</b></li>
  <li>And, eventually, a utility for safer iteration and save handling</li>
</ul>
<br>
If implemented carefully, the Companion can become one of the features that 
helps VitaEngine feel less like a collection of tools and more like a 
<b>cohesive development platform</b>.
<br>
<br>
</h4>
